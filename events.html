SPS DAY TALK

The IEEE SPS Day- Deep Oscillatory Neural Networks For Signal Processing was conducted on 24th May 2024 by the SPS student chapter at the ECE seminar hall. There were 53 participants from BME, ECE department. The event started at 10:00 AM. Dr.V.Srinivasa Chakravarthy, Professor, Dept. of Biotechnology, IIT Madras delved into a cutting-edge intersection of neuroscience-inspired algorithms and signal analysis. Then we were briefed about the advantages of DONNs over traditional neural networks, highlighting their robustness to noisy and non-stationary signals, as well as their potential for learning complex temporal patterns. He also gave a novel approach to processing time-series data, particularly in fields like speech recognition, image processing and biomedical signal analysis. He explained how DONNs utilize deep learning techniques to extract hierarchical features from signals, while also leveraging oscillatory dynamics to enhance model performance. The session was informative and fun and the professor taught good and at a pace we could all keep up and understand.

SHORT COURSE ON “EASY AND SOLID LOGICS FOR RESEARCH PAPER WRITING IN HIGH IMPACT JOURNALS”

Dr. Akihiko (Ken) Sugiyama, currently with Yahoo! JAPAN Research, is a prominent figure in signal processing, particularly in audio coding and noise control. His distinguished career includes a lengthy tenure as a research engineer at NEC Central Research Laboratories and various key roles in the IEEE Signal Processing Society (SPS).

Day 1 began with a slide presentation focused on a three-point analysis, which was elucidated using various examples and demonstrations. Following a tea break, participants were tasked with assignments. They were instructed to independently choose a research paper, analyse its contents, and complete the assignment that consisted of Value, Trick and Problem, thus effectively evaluating the chosen paper. Dr. Sugiyama later reviewed these assignments, addressing any doubts and offering fresh insights and ideas.

On Day 2, the session started with an introduction to the principles of graph design and valuable tips for effective drafting. The participants were then assigned a fresh set of assignments aimed at testing their comprehension and application of the newly acquired knowledge. Additionally, the significance of crafting a well-structured abstract was emphasized, and this critical skill was revisited and reviewed later in the session to ensure its importance was thoroughly understood by all participants.

The short course also had a Distinguished Lecture (DL) on "Personal Information Devices". The DL had over 60 participants from various field of interest. The talk was truly captivating and offered a profound insight into the fascinating history of various devices. It skilfully unravelled the evolution of these devices, providing a comprehensive perspective on how they have transformed over time. Various technologies such as Audio encoding and decoding were explained to the participants.

ICASSP ‘24 - SEOUL

The IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal Processing (ICASSP) 2024, held from April 14 to 19, included a comprehensive program that highlighted advances in biomedical engineering and signal processing.

The conference featured a series of plenary speeches by notable professionals such as Seongwhan Lee, Bhaskar Rao, Daniel Lee, and Jitendra Malik. These sessions presented in-depth insights into the most recent research and technology advances in signal processing, as well as their applications in biomedical engineering.

In addition to plenary sessions, the conference featured a number of workshops and tutorials on biomedical signal processing. These workshops offered hands-on learning opportunities and in-depth talks about medical imaging, health informatics, and machine learning applications in healthcare.

The industry workshops, which included leading organizations such as MathWorks and Meta, enhanced the curriculum by demonstrating realistic implementations and real-world applications of signal processing technology in the biomedical area.

SPS SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM

The application process for the SPS Scholarship Program opens in March every year and remains open until the end of June. Applications are submitted through the IEEE website. The primary eligibility criterion for this program is membership in the IEEE Signal Processing Society.

Under this program, a total of $7,000 is provided as a scholarship in three installments: $2,000 in each of the first two years and $3,000 in the final year. It is recommended to renew the application for the 2nd and 3rd years.

The application includes several questions, such as:

  • The signal processing courses you have completed in past semesters or plan to complete in upcoming semesters (a minimum of 3 subjects).
  • Projects and achievements you have accomplished.
  • Hackathons and competitions you have participated in.
  • The need for this scholarship.
  • Why you believe you are eligible for the program.
  • How you intend to use the scholarship.
  • Where you see yourself in the next 5-10 years.
  • Letters of Recommendation (LOR) from two of your project guides.

I received my first-year scholarship of $2,000 and used it to complete my final year project at NIMHANS, Bangalore. Currently, I have applied for the renewal of the scholarship for the second year.

TWO DAY WORKSHOP ON “MATLAB AND SIMULINK FOR ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE WITH LOW-COST HARDWARE INTERFACING"

The workshop took place at DSP Lab, Department of ECE for a period of two days - 22/08/2023 and 23/08/2023. It aimed to equip students with fundamental insights into the capabilities of MATLAB and Simulink for Al and ML. It was structured to provide a comprehensive understanding and hands-on experience on the first day, which primarily delved into the basic features of MATLAB and Artificial Intelligence. This included practical demonstrations using Machine Learning and Deep Learning toolboxes, in addition to introducing the participants to the available on-ramp courses in MATLAB.

On the second day, the focus shifted towards an overview of hardware support from MATLAB and Simulink. Participants were treated to a demonstration showcasing the interfacing capabilities of MATLAB and Simulink with devices like Arduino and Raspberry Pi. The hands- on sessions were particularly lauded by the attendees, who found them both informative and fascinating.

DHands-on demos on Machine Learning and Deep Learning toolboxes. Introduction to available MATLAB on-ramp courses, Demonstration of MATLAB and Simulink's interface with Arduino and Raspberry Pi. Informative and engaging hands-on sessions left the participants enthused and enlightened.

The workshop was a resounding success, offering a platform for budding engineers to explore the practical applications of MATLAB and Simulink in the realm of Artificial Intelligence while delving into low-cost hardware interfacing.

INAUGURATION OF SSN IEEE SPS - 2023

The Inauguration of the IEEE Signal Processing Society, conducted on August 21, 2023, by the SPS student chapter at the ECE Seminar Hall, marked the beginning of the society's activities for the term. The event, attended by 123 participants from 3rd and 4th Year BME students, commenced at 10:00 AM. Key activities included the swearing-in of newly elected leaders, such as the Chair, Vice Chair, and core Committee members, who outlined their vision and goals for advancing the field and promoting research and education in signal processing.

Dr. M.D. Selvaraj, Professor, Dept. of ECE, IIITDM Kancheepuram delivered an informative talk on the latest advancements in Visible Light Communication. The event also provided networking opportunities for professionals, researchers, and academics, fostering collaborations and the exchange of ideas. The inauguration concluded with a celebratory gathering, allowing attendees to socialize and connect informally.
